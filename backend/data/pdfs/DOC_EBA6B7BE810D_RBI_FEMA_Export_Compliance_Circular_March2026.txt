RESERVE BANK OF INDIA
FOREIGN EXCHANGE MANAGEMENT ACT, 1999

RBI/2025-26/147
A.P. (DIR Series) Circular No. 28
Date: March 20, 2026

Subject: Revised Timelines and Reporting Requirements for Export of Goods and
         Software — SOFTEX Form Submission, Export Realisation, and
         Letter of Undertaking (LUT) Renewal for FY 2026-27

To:
All Authorised Dealer Category-I Banks
All Exporters of Goods and Software
All SEZ Units and STPI-registered Software Exporters

---

1. BACKGROUND

The Reserve Bank of India, in exercise of powers conferred under Section 10(4) and
Section 11(2) of the Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999 (FEMA), and all powers
enabling it in this behalf, has reviewed the existing export compliance framework.
Pursuant to representations received from industry bodies including FIEO, NASSCOM,
and CII regarding operational difficulties, this circular revises certain timelines
and introduces new digital reporting requirements effective April 1, 2026.

All exporters of goods and software holding an Import Export Code (IEC) are required
to note these changes and update their compliance processes accordingly.

---

2. REVISED TIMELINE: SOFTEX FORM SUBMISSION

2.1 Current Requirement
Under the existing framework, exporters of software, IT services, and IT-enabled
services are required to submit SOFTEX forms to the designated Software Technology
Parks of India (STPI) or Special Economic Zone (SEZ) authorities within 30 days of
invoice generation.

2.2 Revised Requirement (Effective April 1, 2026)
The submission timeline for SOFTEX forms is revised as follows:

   a) For invoices up to USD 25,000 (or equivalent): SOFTEX submission within
      30 days of invoice date (no change)

   b) For invoices above USD 25,000 and up to USD 1,00,000: SOFTEX submission
      within 21 days of invoice date (reduced from 30 days)

   c) For invoices above USD 1,00,000: SOFTEX submission within 15 days of
      invoice date (reduced from 30 days)

2.3 New Digital Submission Requirement
With effect from April 1, 2026, all SOFTEX submissions must be made through the
RBI's Export Data Processing and Monitoring System (EDPMS) portal. Physical
submission to STPI offices will be accepted only for invoices below USD 10,000,
and only where the exporter does not have EDPMS access.

2.4 Penalty for Non-Compliance
Failure to submit SOFTEX within the revised timelines shall attract:
   - Compounding penalty under FEMA Section 13: up to 3 times the invoice value
   - In cases of wilful non-submission: export proceeds may be blocked by
     Authorised Dealer banks pending regularisation
   - RBI may direct suspension of IEC for repeat defaulters

---

3. EXPORT REALISATION DEADLINE

3.1 Goods Exporters
The existing timeline for realisation and repatriation of export proceeds for goods
exporters remains 9 months from the date of shipment for all exporters.

Exception: For exports to countries under the ACU (Asian Clearing Union) mechanism,
the timeline remains 6 months from shipment date.

3.2 Software and Services Exporters
For software exports and IT-enabled services, export proceeds must be realised
within 15 months from the invoice date (enhanced from 12 months, effective
April 1, 2026). This enhancement is in recognition of longer billing cycles in
multi-year software contracts.

3.3 Write-Off of Unrealised Export Proceeds
Exporters may write off unrealised export proceeds (without RBI approval) up to
10% of the total export proceeds due in a financial year, subject to:
   i)  The exporter having a satisfactory FEMA compliance record for the preceding
       3 years
   ii) The write-off being reported in the annual SOFTEX reconciliation statement
   iii) AD bank certification that recovery efforts were genuinely made

---

4. LETTER OF UNDERTAKING (LUT) — RENEWAL FOR FY 2026-27

4.1 Mandatory Renewal
All GST-registered exporters who supply goods or services under zero-rated supply
without payment of integrated tax must file a fresh Letter of Undertaking (LUT)
for FY 2026-27 before March 31, 2026.

Exporters who have not renewed their LUT by April 1, 2026 will be required to
either:
   a) Pay IGST at applicable rates on all export invoices and claim refund, OR
   b) Apply for fresh LUT online through the GST portal (Form GST RFD-11)

4.2 Impact of Lapsed LUT
If the LUT lapses and the exporter raises export invoices without IGST payment:
   - GST department may raise demand for IGST on all zero-rated supplies made
     without a valid LUT
   - Interest at 18% per annum from the date of supply
   - Penalty up to 10% of the tax demand under Section 122 of the CGST Act

4.3 Process for LUT Renewal
LUT renewal is entirely online:
   Step 1: Login to GST portal (www.gst.gov.in)
   Step 2: Navigate to Services → User Services → Furnish Letter of Undertaking
   Step 3: Select FY 2026-27 and complete Form RFD-11
   Step 4: Apply DSC or EVC verification
   LUT is auto-approved — no physical submission required.

---

5. NEW REQUIREMENT: ANNUAL FEMA COMPLIANCE CERTIFICATE

5.1 Applicability
With effect from FY 2025-26 (i.e., filing by June 30, 2026), all exporters whose
cumulative export invoices exceed Rs. 5 crore in a financial year are required to
submit an Annual FEMA Compliance Certificate (AFCC) to their AD bank.

5.2 What the AFCC Must Cover
The certificate, signed by a Chartered Accountant, must confirm:
   i)   All export invoices above USD 25,000 were supported by SOFTEX filings
   ii)  Export proceeds were realised within the prescribed timelines
   iii) Write-offs, if any, were within the 10% limit and properly documented
   iv)  No outstanding export bills beyond the realisation deadline exist without
        RBI approval or valid extension

5.3 Penalty for Non-Submission
AD banks are directed to flag exporters who fail to submit the AFCC by June 30,
2026 to their regional RBI office. Non-submission may result in:
   - Suspension of export-related FEMA facilities
   - Requirement for prior RBI approval on future export transactions above
     USD 50,000

---

6. SPECIFIC GUIDANCE FOR COMMON EXPORTER CATEGORIES

6.1 Textile and Manufacturing Exporters
Goods exporters in the textile, garment, and manufacturing sectors making
shipments under Letters of Credit (LC) or Documents against Payment (DP) terms
must ensure all export bills are reported to the AD bank within 21 days of
shipment via the EDPMS system. Non-reporting attracts compounding proceedings
under FEMA.

For exporters with annual export turnover above Rs. 10 crore, the AD bank will
now conduct a quarterly FEMA compliance review — exporters must maintain
invoice-wise SOFTEX reconciliation available for review.

6.2 IT and Software Exporters
Software exporters registered with STPI or operating from SEZs must transition
fully to EDPMS-based SOFTEX filing by June 30, 2026. During the transition period
(April 1 to June 30, 2026), parallel filing through both STPI and EDPMS is
permitted.

Companies with monthly export invoices above USD 50,000 must appoint a dedicated
FEMA compliance officer and register their details with the AD bank by May 31, 2026.

6.3 NBFCs and Financial Services Companies with ECB (External Commercial Borrowings)
NBFCs that have raised External Commercial Borrowings must continue to file monthly
ECB-2 returns with RBI through the EDPMS portal by the 7th of the following month.
No change to this obligation. However, the minimum reporting threshold for ECB-2
is reduced from USD 5 million to USD 1 million effective April 1, 2026, bringing
more NBFCs within the reporting requirement.

---

7. ACTION REQUIRED — SUMMARY FOR ALL EXPORTERS

All entities holding an IEC code must complete the following before April 1, 2026:

   i)   Review all pending SOFTEX submissions and clear any backlog immediately
   ii)  Register on EDPMS portal (edpms.rbi.org.in) if not already registered
   iii) Renew LUT for FY 2026-27 on GST portal before March 31, 2026
   iv)  Identify invoices above USD 25,000 where the revised 21-day/15-day
        SOFTEX timeline will apply from April 1, 2026
   v)   If annual export turnover exceeds Rs. 5 crore, engage CA firm for
        Annual FEMA Compliance Certificate by June 30, 2026
   vi)  For NBFC ECBs: verify if new USD 1 million threshold brings additional
        borrowings within ECB-2 reporting scope

---

8. APPLICABILITY

This circular applies to:
   - All exporters of goods holding an IEC code
   - All exporters of software, IT services, and IT-enabled services
   - SEZ units and STPI-registered entities
   - NBFCs with External Commercial Borrowings
   - Authorised Dealer Category-I banks (for compliance monitoring purposes)

This circular does not apply to:
   - Import transactions
   - Domestic-only businesses without any foreign exchange transactions
   - Individuals making personal remittances under LRS
