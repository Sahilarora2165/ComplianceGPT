1. INTRODUCTION

The Goods and Services Tax Network (GSTN) has developed the Invoice Management
System (IMS) to address the persistent problem of Input Tax Credit (ITC) mismatches
between supplier filings (GSTR-1) and recipient claims (GSTR-3B). The IMS creates
a real-time invoice-level reconciliation dashboard on the GST portal that enables
recipients to accept, reject, or keep invoices in pending status before ITC is
claimed.

Effective April 1, 2026, use of IMS is mandatory for all GST-registered taxpayers
with aggregate annual turnover exceeding Rs. 5 crore in FY 2025-26. Voluntary use
is available for all other registered taxpayers from the same date.

---

2. HOW IMS WORKS

2.1 Supplier Action
When a supplier saves or files their GSTR-1 or GSTR-1A, all B2B invoices are
automatically pushed to the IMS dashboard of the corresponding recipient. The
recipient sees these invoices in real time before filing their GSTR-3B.

2.2 Recipient Actions Available
For each invoice appearing in IMS, the recipient can take one of three actions:

   a) ACCEPT — Confirms the invoice is correct and ITC will be claimed in GSTR-3B
   b) REJECT — Marks the invoice as incorrect; supplier must issue a credit note
   c) PENDING — Defers the decision to a future filing period

2.3 Deemed Acceptance
If no action is taken on an invoice by the time the recipient files their GSTR-3B,
the invoice is automatically deemed ACCEPTED and ITC is included in that return.
This is critical — taxpayers who do not actively manage their IMS dashboard may
inadvertently claim ITC on incorrect invoices.

2.4 Impact on GSTR-3B
From April 1, 2026, the ITC auto-populated in Table 4 of GSTR-3B will be sourced
exclusively from IMS-accepted invoices. Manual entry of ITC amounts in GSTR-3B
that are not backed by IMS-accepted invoices will be flagged as discrepancies
and may trigger GST department scrutiny.

---

3. MANDATORY RECONCILIATION REQUIREMENTS

3.1 Monthly IMS Reconciliation
All taxpayers covered under mandatory IMS must complete invoice reconciliation
on the IMS dashboard by the 14th of the following month. For example, all
April 2026 invoices must be acted upon (accepted/rejected/pending) by May 14, 2026.

3.2 Quarterly Reconciliation Statement (Form GSTR-IMS-Q)
A new quarterly reconciliation statement — Form GSTR-IMS-Q — is introduced with
effect from April 1, 2026. This must be filed within 30 days of the end of each quarter:

   Quarter | Period       | Filing Deadline
   Q1      | Apr-Jun 2026 | July 30, 2026
   Q2      | Jul-Sep 2026 | October 30, 2026
   Q3      | Oct-Dec 2026 | January 30, 2027
   Q4      | Jan-Mar 2027 | April 30, 2027

3.3 Reconciliation with GSTR-2B
IMS data feeds directly into GSTR-2B (auto-drafted ITC statement). Taxpayers must
ensure that the ITC claimed in GSTR-3B does not exceed the ITC available in
GSTR-2B generated from IMS-accepted invoices. Excess claims will be automatically
reversed by the GST system with interest at 24% per annum under Section 50(3).

---

4. ITC REVERSAL AND PENALTY PROVISIONS

4.1 ITC on Rejected Invoices
If ITC has already been claimed on an invoice that is subsequently rejected in IMS,
the ITC must be reversed in the same month's GSTR-3B. Interest at 18% per annum
under Section 50(1) applies from the date of original claim to the date of reversal.

4.2 ITC on Non-Existent Invoices
Where ITC is claimed on invoices that do not appear in IMS (i.e., invoices not
uploaded by the supplier), the recipient:
   - Must not claim ITC on such invoices from April 1, 2026
   - For existing claims: reversal required in the April 2026 GSTR-3B
   - Interest at 24% per annum under Section 50(3) for excess claims

4.3 Late Filing of GSTR-IMS-Q
Non-filing or late filing of the quarterly Form GSTR-IMS-Q attracts:
   - Late fee of Rs. 50 per day (Rs. 25 CGST + Rs. 25 SGST)
   - Maximum late fee: Rs. 5,000 per return (Rs. 2,500 CGST + Rs. 2,500 SGST)

4.4 Repeat Non-Compliance
Where a taxpayer fails to reconcile IMS and claims excess ITC for two or more
consecutive quarters, the GST officer may:
   - Issue a Show Cause Notice under Section 73 or 74
   - Block Input Tax Credit under Rule 86A
   - Suspend GST registration under Section 29(2)

5. SPECIFIC GUIDANCE BY BUSINESS TYPE

5.1 Manufacturing and Export Companies
Manufacturing companies claiming ITC on raw material purchases must ensure all
supplier invoices are accepted in IMS before claiming ITC in GSTR-3B. For export-
oriented units claiming refunds under the zero-rated supply mechanism, IMS
acceptance is now a prerequisite for refund processing. Refund applications that
do not match IMS-accepted invoices will be rejected automatically.

Companies with large vendor bases (50+ suppliers) are advised to use GSTN's bulk
action feature in IMS to accept/reject multiple invoices simultaneously.

5.2 Restaurants and Food Service Businesses
Food service businesses registered under the composition scheme are not covered
under mandatory IMS. However, regular scheme registrants (turnover > Rs. 5 crore)
in food service must comply. Key practical implication: purchases from unregistered
vendors (common in the food industry) will not appear in IMS and ITC cannot be
claimed on such purchases regardless.

5.3 IT and Software Companies
IT companies making B2B software sales must ensure their GSTR-1 is filed on time
as delays push invoices late into the recipient's IMS dashboard, potentially causing
the recipient to defer ITC claims. For contracts where advance payments are made,
the tax invoice date (not advance receipt date) governs the IMS upload timing.

IT exporters claiming GST refunds on zero-rated supplies must note: IMS acceptance
by the overseas recipient is not applicable (exports are zero-rated). However,
domestic B2B supply invoices must be managed through IMS.

5.4 NBFCs and Financial Services
NBFCs and financial services companies that are primarily exempt from GST (on
interest income) but have some taxable supplies must carefully track IMS. The
partial ITC reversal under Rule 42 (proportionate reversal for exempt supplies)
must be recalculated based on IMS-accepted invoices only, not total purchases.

5.5 Stock Broking Firms
Stock broking companies receiving brokerage income (taxable at 18%) must manage
IMS for all input services — office rent, software subscriptions, professional
fees, exchange connectivity charges. These inputs are fully eligible for ITC
provided they appear in IMS and are accepted. Broking firms with sub-offices
should ensure all GST registrations are linked for consolidated IMS management.

---

6. TRANSITION PROVISIONS (APRIL 1 — JUNE 30, 2026)

6.1 Backlog Clearance
All outstanding unreconciled invoices from October 2024 onwards that appear in the
legacy GSTR-2B but have not been actioned in IMS must be reviewed and accepted or
rejected in the IMS dashboard by May 31, 2026.

6.2 Training and System Readiness
GSTN has made available a sandbox environment for testing IMS workflows at
sandbox.gstn.gov.in. All large taxpayers (turnover > Rs. 50 crore) are required
to complete IMS readiness testing in the sandbox and submit a readiness certificate
to their jurisdictional GST officer by April 30, 2026.

6.3 Waiver of Late Fee During Transition
For the first quarter (April-June 2026), late fees for Form GSTR-IMS-Q will be
waived provided the return is filed by August 15, 2026 (45-day extension). This
waiver applies only to the first quarter.

---

7. ACTION REQUIRED

All GST-registered taxpayers with turnover above Rs. 5 crore must:

   i)   Log into the GST portal and access the IMS dashboard immediately
   ii)  Clear all pending invoices from October 2024 to March 2026 by May 31, 2026
   iii) Configure accounting software to pull IMS data for monthly reconciliation
   iv)  Train accounts payable team on accept/reject/pending workflow
   v)   Review and reverse any ITC claimed on invoices not appearing in IMS
   vi)  Complete GSTN sandbox readiness testing by April 30, 2026 (if turnover > Rs. 50 crore)
   vii) File Form GSTR-IMS-Q for Q1 FY 2026-27 by July 30, 2026

---

8. APPLICABILITY

This advisory is mandatory for:
   - All regular scheme GST taxpayers with aggregate annual turnover > Rs. 5 crore
   - All taxpayers claiming GST refunds under zero-rated supply mechanism

This advisory is voluntary (but recommended) for:
   - GST taxpayers with turnover below Rs. 5 crore

This advisory does not apply to:
   - Composition scheme taxpayers
   - Persons registered solely for TDS/TCS under GST
   - Input Service Distributors (ISDs)
