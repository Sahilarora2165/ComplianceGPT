1. BACKGROUND

The Finance Act 2026 has amended the TDS provisions under Chapter XVII-B of the
Income Tax Act, 1961. This circular provides consolidated guidance on the revised
TDS rates, enhanced threshold limits, and new compliance obligations effective
April 1, 2026 (Assessment Year 2027-28).

All tax deductors holding a valid TAN are required to note these changes and update
their deduction logic before the first payment of FY 2026-27.

---

2. REVISED TDS RATES EFFECTIVE APRIL 1, 2026

2.1 Section 194J — Professional and Technical Services

The TDS rate under Section 194J is revised as follows:

  a) Fees for Professional Services (doctors, lawyers, architects, accountants):
     Rate revised from 10% to 10% (no change)
     Threshold: Enhanced from Rs. 30,000 to Rs. 50,000 per annum

  b) Fees for Technical Services (IT, software, engineering consultancy):
     Rate revised from 2% to 2% (no change)
     Threshold: Enhanced from Rs. 30,000 to Rs. 50,000 per annum

  c) Royalty payments (software licenses, IP usage fees):
     Rate: 10%
     Threshold: Rs. 50,000 per annum (enhanced from Rs. 30,000)

Key clarification: Payments to IT and software companies for software development,
maintenance contracts, and SaaS subscriptions where a service element is predominant
shall continue to be classified under Section 194J(b) at 2%, NOT under Section 194C.
This clarification resolves prior ambiguity flagged in CBDT Circular 7/2024.

2.2 Section 194C — Payments to Contractors and Sub-Contractors

  Rate: 1% (Individual/HUF) | 2% (others — companies, firms, LLPs)
  Single transaction threshold: Enhanced from Rs. 30,000 to Rs. 50,000
  Annual aggregate threshold: Enhanced from Rs. 1,00,000 to Rs. 1,50,000

Applicable to: manufacturing contracts, logistics, facility management, event
management, advertising agencies, and all work contracts. NOT applicable to
IT service agreements covered under 194J above.

2.3 Section 194H — Commission and Brokerage

  Rate: 5% (no change)
  Threshold: Enhanced from Rs. 15,000 to Rs. 20,000 per annum

Applicable to: stock brokers, insurance agents, travel agents, real estate brokers,
and all other commission-earning intermediaries. Stock broking firms paying sub-broker
commissions must deduct at source under this section.

2.4 Section 194I — Rent

  Rent for land, building, furniture, fittings: 10%
  Rent for plant, machinery, equipment: 2%
  Threshold: Enhanced from Rs. 2,40,000 to Rs. 3,00,000 per annum

This change directly impacts all businesses paying office rent above Rs. 25,000
per month. Deductors who have not been deducting TDS because the annual rent
was below the old Rs. 2,40,000 threshold must now reassess their obligation.

---

3. NEW OBLIGATION: QUARTERLY TDS RECONCILIATION CERTIFICATE

With effect from April 1, 2026, all deductors with TDS deductions exceeding
Rs. 5 lakh in the preceding financial year must file a Quarterly TDS
Reconciliation Certificate (Form 26QC-A) within 30 days of the close of each
quarter. This is in addition to the existing quarterly TDS return (Form 24Q/26Q).

Non-filing of Form 26QC-A attracts a penalty of Rs. 500 per day under the newly
inserted Section 272A(2)(k), subject to a maximum of Rs. 1,00,000 per quarter.

Quarter | Period        | Due Date for Return Filing
Q1      | Apr–Jun 2026  | July 31, 2026
Q2      | Jul–Sep 2026  | October 31, 2026
Q3      | Oct–Dec 2026  | January 31, 2027
Q4      | Jan–Mar 2027  | May 31, 2027

TDS deposit due dates remain unchanged:
  - Government deductors: same day of deduction
  - Non-government deductors: 7th of the following month (March: 30th April)

5. CONSEQUENCE OF NON-COMPLIANCE

5.1 Short Deduction or Non-Deduction
Where TDS is not deducted or is deducted at a lower rate, the deductor is treated
as an assessee-in-default under Section 201. Interest at 1% per month is charged
from the date on which TDS was deductible to the date of actual deduction, and
interest at 1.5% per month from the date of deduction to the date of deposit.

5.2 Late Filing of TDS Returns
Fee of Rs. 200 per day under Section 234E, with a maximum up to the amount of
TDS involved. In addition, penalty under Section 271H may be levied up to
Rs. 1,00,000 in cases of inaccurate information.

5.3 Non-Issue of TDS Certificate (Form 16/16A)
Penalty of Rs. 100 per day under Section 272A(2)(g) for delay in issue of TDS
certificates to deductees. Form 16 for salaried employees must be issued by
June 15 of the assessment year. Form 16A must be issued within 15 days of the
due date of filing the quarterly TDS return.

---

6. SPECIFIC GUIDANCE FOR COMMON BUSINESS SCENARIOS

6.1 IT and Software Companies (Exporters)
Software export companies receiving payments from overseas clients: TDS is not
applicable on receipts. However, when making payments to Indian sub-contractors,
freelancers, or technical consultants within India, TDS under 194J(b) at 2% applies.
Such companies must also ensure Form 26AS reconciliation is complete before filing
advance tax for Q4 FY 2026-27.

6.2 NBFCs and Financial Services Companies
NBFCs paying interest on deposits, borrowings, or subordinated debt must continue
to deduct TDS under Section 194A at 10% (PAN available) or 20% (PAN not available).
Additionally, NBFCs must ensure TDS on all professional fee payments (legal, audit,
actuarial) is updated to the enhanced threshold of Rs. 50,000 effective April 1.

6.3 Stock Broking Firms
Sub-broker commissions paid by broking firms must be assessed against the revised
Section 194H threshold of Rs. 20,000. Firms whose sub-brokers earned commissions
between Rs. 15,000 and Rs. 20,000 in FY 2025-26 who were previously exempt are
now clearly exempt going forward but must verify FY 2025-26 deductions were correct.

6.4 Individuals and Professionals Under Presumptive Taxation
Professionals under Section 44ADA or 44AD who receive payments subject to TDS need
not take any action — TDS is the obligation of the payer. However, they must ensure
their Form 26AS reflects all TDS deducted by clients before filing ITR for AY 2026-27.
Mismatch between Form 26AS and ITR schedule TDS remains a primary trigger for
scrutiny notices under Section 143(2).

---

7. ACTION REQUIRED

All tax deductors must:

  i)   Update payroll and accounts software with revised thresholds before April 1, 2026
  ii)  Review all vendor contracts to reclassify payments under correct TDS sections
  iii) Obtain Form 15G/15H from eligible vendors to avoid excess deduction
  iv)  File any pending TDS returns for Q3 FY 2025-26 (due January 31, 2026)
       immediately — interest and penalty are accruing daily
  v)   Issue Form 16A for Q3 within 15 days of filing the Q3 return
  vi)  Enroll for the new Form 26QC-A filing obligation if aggregate TDS exceeds
       Rs. 5 lakh in FY 2025-26

---

8. APPLICABILITY

This circular is applicable to all persons responsible for deducting tax at source
under Chapter XVII-B of the Income Tax Act, 1961, including:
  - Companies (public and private)
  - Limited Liability Partnerships
  - Partnership firms
  - Individuals and HUFs whose accounts are subject to audit under Section 44AB
  - Government offices and PSUs

This circular does not apply to individuals and HUFs not required to get their
accounts audited, unless they are paying rent under Section 194-IB.
